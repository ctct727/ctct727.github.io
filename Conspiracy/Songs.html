Paul McCartney
is Dead
Songs

Believers in this theory say that there are multiple instances of the Beatles hinting at Paul's death through the music they made. However, most of these clues have been disproven one way or another.


"Sgt. Pepper's Lonely Hearts Club Band"

Towards the end of the song "Sgt. Pepper's Lonely Hearts Club Band" in the background someone sounds like they're shouting "Paul is dead, really really dead!"

"He blew his mind out in a car"

In the song "A Day in the Life" written by John Lennon, one verse is all about a car crash which is believed to be a reference to the crash where supposedly Paul died.

The mumbling towards the end of the song if played backwards also sounds like someone is saying "Paul is dead, miss him, miss him"

"Good Morning, Good Morning"

In the song "Good Morning, Good Morning" they sing, "nothing to do to save his life... and you're on your own/you're in the street... people running at about 5 o'clock"
